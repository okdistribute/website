karissa mckelvey

bio

Karissa McKelvey is an engineering professional, public interest technologist, and researcher working in solidarity with vulnerable populations around the globe. Her work has been depended upon by at-risk users including environmental & human rights defenders, journalists, and civil society activists. Karissa's perspectives and work has been featured in high-profile news outlets such as the New York Times, Wall Street Journal, and NPR.

In 2019, she was a fellow at the Aspen Institute's Tech Policy Hub, where she advocated for ethical tech policy in the California criminal justice system and co-published a policy brief on data-driven policing in Florida schools. She is an expert on cooperative and sustainable practices in open source. In 2016, she co-founded Code for Science and Society to systematize positive outcomes across the ecosystem of open source public interest technology. Since 2014, she has focused on radically re-imagining the Internet to prioritize safety, security, and access.
